About

Systems leadership that executives can govern.

Jay Paulson is a senior engineering leader, speaker, and advisor known for connecting architecture, systems behavior, and delivery maturity to the outcomes executive teams need to trust.

Executive Bio

Jay brings more than 25 years of engineering and technology leadership experience across platform modernization, organizational scale, and delivery transformation. He has led engineering organizations in the 50 to 80+ range, shaped programs with $300M+ platform impact, unlocked $15M in savings, and contributed to $100M+ in strategic program wins.

His work sits at the intersection of systems leadership, architecture-led transformation, and flow-based operating design. Rather than treating predictability as an operations clean-up exercise, Jay frames it as an engineering system problem: shaped by architecture, work design, visibility, and the quality of decisions leadership can make from the signal they receive.

Leadership Lens

  • Architecture-led transformation that improves leverage, not just compliance
  • Kanban and flow-based systems thinking applied at an executive operating level
  • Engineering behavior linked clearly to business outcomes, risk posture, and investment confidence
  • Visibility models that help executives govern technology without distorting team behavior
